# The Almanac — working vocabulary

**Admission rule:** a term enters only after it has changed real decisions;
ornamental entries get pruned. One home per truth: when a term is law, the law
lives in `agent-roles.md` and the almanac only points at it. (This is why
Grounds Crew/Fauna aren't here, and why unbuilt organs — mycelium,
pollination — wait until they exist to do the carrying.)

- **Water table** — the append-only `events` stream. All attention and usage
  (syncs, copies, stars, views) percolates down into it; trending, reputation,
  and discovery draw it back up. **Nothing pumps uphill**: gaming trending,
  paid placement, astroturfed stars are irrigation fraud, forbidden by the
  physics of the metaphor.
- **Quarantine greenhouse** — `data/seed/wishlist.jsonl`. Specimens we covet
  but cannot legally plant until the license clears. Zero content crosses the
  quarantine line — name + URL + reason only.
- **Phytosanitary gate** — the inspections on `data/seed`: `seed-audit.yml`
  (byte-exactness vs pinned upstream) plus the Steward's manual audit
  (completeness, licenses). Nothing enters the corpus uninspected. Known
  missing inspection: **blight** screening — byte-perfect, properly-licensed
  copies of *malicious* content would pass today's gates.
- **Cold frame** — scratch-Postgres verification under controlled glass before
  anything touches a real DB (the v2 smoke, the import-seed double-run, the
  PR #12 update probe). Nothing is planted in the open bed first.
- **Pollen doesn't re-enter** — marketing material leaves the atrium
  (exports, the open-sourced MCP server, syndication) and never writes back
  to the corpus, the DB, or the repo. Attraction over pursuit.
- **Cuttings** — copy-to-library, taken under the asset's license.
  `arr` = specimen behind glass: admire, don't propagate
  (`allowsCopy: false` in the registry).
- **The no-trees rule** — arboreal vocabulary (branch, trunk, root) belongs
  to git in any repository. The garden speaks in shrubs, vines, fungi, and
  beds; the only tree here is the one git planted.
